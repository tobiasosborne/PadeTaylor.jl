## The ℘ pole lattice (analytic)

The equianharmonic ℘ (`g₂ = 0`) has primitive periods `2ω` and
`2ω·e^{iπ/3}`. The poles of `u(z) = ℘(z+c₁)` with `c₁ = -1` therefore
sit at

    z(m,n) = 1 + 2ω(m + n/2) + i·ω√3·n,    m,n ∈ ℤ.

`n = 0` is FW's real-axis row (`x = 1 + 2ωk`, md:297); `n = ±1` are the
rows at `Im z = ±ω√3 ≈ ±2.36` visible in FW's figure. No numerical
pole-finding is needed for Fig 5.1 — the lattice is closed-form.

## Empirical results

  - **Fig 5.1** — 36 ℘ poles fall in the displayed window
    (`2ω = 2.7261`). The `path_network_solve` walk to z = 30 takes
    **75 visited nodes** and lands `u(30)` with **rel-err `1.65e-13`**
    (`Float64`); FW report `7.62e-14` at `BF`-256, so the `Float64`
    figure is within ~2× of FW's extended-precision number. The
    rendered path weaves through the low-`|u|` channel between the
    real-axis pole row and the `Im z = -ω√3` row — a close match to
    FW's `_page_12_Figure_4.jpeg`.
  - **Fig 5.2** — the 24×20 sweep (480 ℘ walks) runs in **~17 s**. The
    best accuracy over the plane is **`log₁₀`(rel-err) `= -14.04` at
    `(order, h) = (30, 0.40)`** — the order axis lands *exactly* on
    FW's standard `order = 30`. At FW's full standard `(30, 0.5)` the
    sweep records `log₁₀`(rel-err) `= -12.78` (rel-err `1.7e-13`) at
    `0.012 s` wall. Panel (a)'s surface has the FW shape: a
    rel-err-`≈1` plateau in the low-order / high-`h` corner, dropping
    off a cliff to `≈ -14` in the accurate region. Panel (b)'s
    smoothed accuracy contours sweep diagonally up-right (more order
    needed as `h` grows), with `(30, 0.5)` sitting in the
    well-spaced favourable region — FW's central claim, reproduced.

## Frictions

  - **`path_network_solve` ignores `prob.order` — the first sweep was
    silently all-order-30.** `path_network_solve(prob, grid; order=30,
    …)` carries its *own* `order` kwarg and never reads the
    `PadeTaylorProblem`'s `order` field. The first Fig 5.2 run set
    `order` only on the problem constructor, so every one of the 480
    cells actually ran at `order = 30` — and the "sweep" reported
    `order = 4` as tied-best, contradicting the whole figure. Fixed by
    passing `order` explicitly to `path_network_solve` in both §5
    scripts. This is a genuine API footgun (a constructor field that
    a downstream driver silently ignores); filed as bead
    `padetaylor-9xf` (P3) — and `fw2011_fig_3_1/4_3/4_4.jl` are
    correct only by the coincidence `ORDER == 30 ==` the kwarg
    default, worth tidying when that bead is taken.
  - **Per-cell wall time is noise-dominated.** At `~0.01–0.05 s` per
    walk the measured time is mostly scheduler/GC jitter, so the raw
    equal-time contours were unusably wiggly. FW md:326 explicitly
    smooths the sweep data before drawing the Fig 5.2b contours
    ("some fine-scale 'jitter' … was smoothed out"); we do the same —
    a 3×3 box smoother on both fields for panel (b), while panel (a)
    keeps the raw surface (FW shows the raw data there too). One raw
    near-failure cell still spikes in panel (a); that is honest
    jitter, consistent with FW's own raw surface.
