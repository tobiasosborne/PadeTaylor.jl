## Ground truth

- `references/markdown/FW2011_painleve_methodology_JCP230/FW2011_painleve_methodology_JCP230.md:202-208`
  — FW 2011 §3.2.2 + eq. (3.3). Away from poles a meromorphic
  `u(z) = u(x,y)` is harmonic (`u_xx + u_yy = 0`); the residual of the
  5-point cross stencil `[1,1,−4,1,1]/h²` is `O(h²)` and tiny in
  smooth regions but large inside pole fields. Fig 3.3 plots
  `log₁₀|Δu|`; FW selects contour level `0.001` (in the plotted
  `log₁₀|Δu|` units) as "a suitable pole field edge description". FW
  also notes (md:208) that "low level 'ridges' appear within the flat
  areas" because neighbouring lattice entries can be reached by
  different paths and then amplified by the `1/h²`.
- `...md:224-227` — eq. (4.1): the precise tritronquée ICs
  `u(0) = -0.1875543083404949`, `u'(0) = 0.3049055602612289`,
  obtained by FW with a 32-digit Maple BVP solve.
- `src/EdgeDetector.jl` — Phase 12.5 (`padetaylor-c2p`, worklog 011):
  `laplacian_residual` (eq. 3.3 verbatim, boundary cells → `NaN`) and
  `pole_field_mask` (default `level = 0.001`, thresholding
  `log₁₀|Δu| > level`).

## What changed

### `figures/fw2011_fig_3_3.jl` (new)

Solves PI `u'' = 6u² + z` with the eq. (4.1) tritronquée ICs on a
uniform `49×49` lattice (spacing `0.25`) over the near-origin window
`x ∈ [-4,8], y ∈ [-6,6]`, via `path_network_solve` with
`enforce_real_axis_symmetry = true`. The lattice is flattened x-major
so `reshape(_, ny, nx)` yields the rows-index-y / cols-index-x
orientation `laplacian_residual` documents. `laplacian_residual` is
applied with the **lattice spacing** (`0.25`) as its `h` — not the
path-network step `0.5`. The `log₁₀|Δu|` surface (interior cells, the
1-cell `NaN` stencil border trimmed) is drawn as an `Axis3` surface +
faint wireframe, with the level-`0.001` contour overlaid via
`contour!` so it sits on the surface at its true height — FW's "level
curve 0.001". The script also prints `pole_field_mask`'s flagged-cell
count as a numerical companion.

No `src/` or `test/` change — the test suite is unchanged from
worklog 022's **1432/1432 GREEN**.

## Empirical results

`49×49` lattice, `h_step = 0.5`, `order = 30`, ran in **4.1 s** (277
visited tree nodes; all 2401 lattice points finite).

  - `log₁₀|Δu|` over the interior: **min `−5.84`, max `4.31`, median
    `−2.64`**. The deep flat smooth plain and the sharp pole-field
    ridges are ~10 orders of magnitude apart in `|Δu|`.
  - `pole_field_mask` at `level = 0.001`: **540 / 2209 interior cells
    flagged** as pole-field (~24%), occupying the back-right
    (positive-real-axis) sector — consistent with the tritronquée's
    single pole field and FW's Fig 3.3 layout.

The first cut used the cruder Fig.-3.1 ICs (`-0.1875 / 0.3049`):
median `log₁₀|Δu|` came out `−0.65` with 38% of the window flagged,
because those ICs place *displaced* pole fields in four sectors that
intrude into the `[-4,8]×[-6,6]` window. Switching to the eq. (4.1)
tritronquée ICs dropped the median to `−2.64` and produced the single
clean smooth plain FW's figure shows — see Frictions.

## Frictions

  - **"near-tritronquée" was ambiguous, and the wrong reading filled
    the window with poles.** FW's Fig 3.3 caption says "near-tritronquée
    … cf. Figs. 1.1c and 3.1" — but Fig 1.1c is the *tritronquée*
    (four sectors pole-free to infinity) and Fig 3.1 is the *near*-tritronquée
    (four sectors with displaced pole fields). The figure itself —
    one pole field, one large smooth plain — is unambiguously the
    tritronquée signature, so the script uses the precise eq. (4.1)
    ICs. Lesson in the spirit of CLAUDE.md Rule 1's "ground truth":
    when a caption's words are ambiguous, the figure's *content* is
    the tiebreaker — read what it shows, not just what it says.
  - **Two different `h`'s in one figure.** `laplacian_residual` takes
    the *lattice spacing* (`0.25`) as its stencil `h`; the
    path-network's *step length* is the unrelated `0.5`. Easy to
    conflate; the script names them `DGRID` and `H_STEP` and comments
    the distinction at the call site.
