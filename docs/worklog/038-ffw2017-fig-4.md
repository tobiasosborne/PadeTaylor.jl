## Ground truth read first

Per Law 1, foundational reads were FFW 2017 §3.1 + Figure 4 caption:

  - **md:200-209** — §3.1 "A tronquée P_V solution".  FFW md:209
    states the existence theorem: "for a given set of parameters
    `α, β, γ, δ` with `δ ≠ 0`, there is a unique tronquée P_V
    solution with `u(z) ~ -1`, `z → ∞` for `-π < arg z < π`.  The
    solution in Figure 4 is such a solution and its ICs were
    obtained by substituting `u = Σ a_n z^{-n}` into P_V and
    evaluating the truncated expansion far out on the positive real
    axis."

  - **md:236** — Figure 4 caption (verbatim):
    > "A tronquée P_V solution in the ζ and z planes with parameters
    > `(α, β, γ, δ) = (1, 0, 1/4, -1/2)` and ICs
    > `u(30) = -1.05294551349665` and
    > `u'(30) = 2.47019460566845e-3` in the z-plane.  The third
    > column is a phase portrait of the solution shown in the second
    > column.  The symmetry-based error estimates for the solution
    > on sheets 0-2 are **3e-10, 7e-7 and 1e-6**, respectively."

  - **md:207** — Table 4 + caption: "all the zeros of the solution
    have double multiplicity.  They can be identified on the phase
    portraits in the third column as points around which each color
    is assumed twice in the order indicated by the color wheel above
    Figure 3 (red→yellow→green etc. for a counterclockwise
    traversal around the point)."  The qualitative feature
    distinguishing PV tronquée from Fig 6's generic PV.

  - **md:45-47** — PV exponential transform `z = exp(ζ)`,
    `u(z) = w(ζ)` (no rescale, unlike PIII).  Same transform used in
    Fig 6 (worklog 036); reused verbatim here.

  - **md:103** — PV strip width `2π` per sheet (vs PIII's `4π`).
    Three sheets `Im ζ ∈ (-π + 2π s, π + 2π s]` for `s ∈ {0, 1, 2}`.

  - **md:72** — "pole density will increase rapidly on the region
    Re ζ ≫ 0".  Quantitative pin in FF4.1.6.

The PV transformed RHS factory `pV_transformed_rhs` and IC mapping
`pV_z_to_ζ` ship in `src/CoordTransforms.jl` from worklog 017 — no
new code in `src/`, this is **pure figure + test wiring** on top of
ADR-0011 (adaptive Padé `h`, worklog 034) + ADR-0012 (non-uniform
Stage-1 nodes, worklog 035) + worklog 037's Cartesian-resample
rendering helper.

## What shipped

### `figures/ffw2017_fig_4.jl` (new, ~280 LOC inc. ~110 LOC docstring)

Builds the P̃_V problem via `pV_transformed_rhs(1, 0, 1/4, -1/2)`
on the FFW md:47 RHS.  Maps the FFW md:236 IC
`(z₀, u₀, u'₀) = (30, -1.05294551349665, 2.47019460566845e-3)` to
the ζ-frame:

    pV_z_to_ζ(30, -1.0529..., 2.47e-3)
      → (ζ₀, w₀, w'₀)
      = (log 30, -1.0529..., 30·2.47e-3)
      = (3.4011973816621555, -1.05294551349665, 0.0741058381700535)

Walks via `path_network_solve` with `step_size_policy = :adaptive_ffw`,
`adaptive_tol = 1e-10`, `k_conservative = 1e-3`, and
`node_separation = R` where `R(ζ) = max(0.05, (4 - Re ζ)/10)` —
Fig 6's prescription, scaled for `Re ζ ∈ [-1, 4]`.

**ζ-window**: `Re ζ ∈ [-1, 4]`, `Im ζ ∈ (-π+0.01, 5π-0.01]`.  The IC at
`ζ₀ ≈ 3.40` sits at the high-Re end of this window; the walker
propagates **inward** (toward decreasing Re ζ) into the pole-free
sector AND outward to sheets 1, 2 above.  Stage-1 target count
6883 (R-spaced rectangular raster); visited tree 5326 nodes after
the walk (~89% Stage-2 coverage on a `120×220` ζ-grid).

**Stage-1 / Stage-2 split** (worklog 037 pattern): walker walks only
the Stage-1 targets, then a manual Stage 2 reads the visited tree
to populate the finer evaluation lattice + the Cartesian z-grid.
Avoids the worklog 037 friction where adding the eval lattice to
the walker's target list forces it through pole spirals.

**Rendering — three columns, three sheets, nine panels**:

  - **Column 1 — `|w(ζ)|` ζ-plane heatmap** per sheet (3 panels).
    Sheet 0 (top-left of layout): visible pole-free smooth plain on
    the LEFT side (low Re ζ, where `|u| ≈ 1` per the tronquée
    asymptote).  Sheets 1 & 2: dense oblique pole lines, the FFW
    md:283 pattern.

  - **Column 2 — `|u(z)|` z-plane modulus** per sheet.  Cartesian
    `Re z, Im z ∈ [-57.3, 57.3]` (5% pad on `|z|_max = exp(4) ≈ 54.6`),
    `300 × 300` resolution, **bilinear interpolation from the
    ζ-grid** (the worklog 037 B2 upgrade — clean smooth surfaces,
    no polar-scatter spoke artefacts).  PV transform `u(z) = w(ζ)`
    means no rescale: directly read off `w` at `ζ = log|z| + i arg z
    + 2π i s` for sheet `s`.

  - **Column 3 — `arg u(z)` z-plane phase portrait** per sheet.
    Same Cartesian z-grid; HSV-encoded `arg u ∈ [-π, π]`.  Sheet 0's
    portrait dominated by red (since `arg(-1) = π` → red on the
    HSV wheel) over the pole-free sector — consistent with the
    `u → -1` asymptote.  Sheets 1, 2 show the full color wheel
    rotating around each pole + zero.  Double-multiplicity zeros
    (FFW md:207) would appear as "color-wheel-twice" points; we
    do not explicitly pin them but they are visible in the layout.

Output `figures/output/ffw2017_fig_4.png` at 1500×1100 px.

### Mutation-proof bites (verified, footer-documented)

  - **M1** — corrupt IC by 2e-3 (`u(30) = -1.05094...` instead of
    `-1.05294...`).  The walker integrates a slightly different
    solution; the captured tight-tol baselines (taken at the
    original IC) no longer match.
    Bite: **FF4.1.3 |Δ| = 3.9e-3** (threshold 7e-6), **FF4.1.4
    |Δ| = 2.7e-2** (threshold 1e-5).  FF4.1.5 sector survives (the
    pole-free signature is robust to small IC perturbations).

  - **M2** — swap `γ ↔ -δ` to `(α,β,γ,δ) = (1, 0, 1/2, -1/4)`.
    Wrong tronquée — the asymptotic-series at `z = 30` doesn't
    satisfy the mutated equation, so the walker integrates a
    *non*-tronquée solution.
    Bite: **FF4.1.3 |Δ| = 0.73** (threshold 7e-6), **FF4.1.4
    |Δ| = 2.21** (threshold 1e-5).  FF4.1.5 sector survives by
    coincidence — `u(3.5+0i) = -1.111` still has `Re u < -0.5`.
    The absolute baseline pins are the load-bearing M2 detectors.

  - **M3** — `step_size_policy = :fixed`, `h = 0.5`, drop
    `node_separation`.  Fixed-h walker produces a coarse visited
    tree (328 nodes vs baseline 5945 — ~18× fewer); sheet-1/2
    samples land on visited nodes too far from the true solution.
    Bite: **FF4.1.3 |Δ| = 1.58** (threshold 7e-6), **FF4.1.4
    |Δ| = 2.26** (threshold 1e-5).  FF4.1.5 survives — the
    principal-sheet sector is short-path from the IC and robust to
    controller choice.

All three mutations bite **FF4.1.3 AND FF4.1.4** at the absolute
baseline pins.  Distinct assertion classes (IC pin + cross-sheet
absolute pins + qualitative sector pin + density gradient) close
orthogonal mutation spaces.

## Frictions

### The loose-vs-tight oracle's gap, redux

The bead spec called out FFW Table 2's per-sheet errors `3e-10 / 7e-7
/ 1e-6` and prescribed loose-vs-tight self-cross-checks with
thresholds one order looser.  Empirically:

  - **Sheet 0 near IC** (`ζ = 3.0 + 0.5i`, ~0.58 from `ζ₀ ≈ 3.40`):
    loose-vs-tight |Δ| = **0.0** — works perfectly.
  - **Sheet 0 far** (`ζ = 0.0 + 0.5i`, ~3.4 from IC): |Δ| = **1.5e-3** —
    already structural noise once we leave the IC neighborhood.
  - **Sheets 1 and 2** (any sample point): |Δ| ∈ **[1e-3, 0.2]** — the
    tree-topology dependence dominates.

This is the worklog 037 §"loose-vs-tight oracle's gap" pattern:
loose and tight walker trees diverge structurally (the controller
accepts different step magnitudes), and at off-IC sheets in pole-
dense regions, the Stage-2 nearest-visited lookup lands on
**totally different visited nodes** between the two runs.  The
|Δ| is *tree-topology noise*, not tolerance error.

Per Rule 2 ("All bugs are deep — investigate root causes"), the
principled fix is to switch oracles, not to relax thresholds.
Worklog 037's solution: use **captured tight-tol absolute baselines**
for off-IC sheet pins (matches FF6.1.5's two-pin design).  The
captured baseline pin tests *physical correctness* — bites
parameter / IC / equation mutations — and gives meaningful FFW-
threshold numbers (FFW's `7e-7 / 1e-6` for sheets 1 / 2 are
**physical-accuracy** numbers from their conjugate-symmetry oracle,
not loose-vs-tight numbers either).

Adopted for FF4.1.3 / FF4.1.4.

### Sample-point selection for sheet 2

First sheet-2 probe was `ζ = 3.0 + (3π+0.5)i` (just past the strip
boundary, per the bead spec).  At tight tol the visited tree's
coverage thins out near this boundary — the probe came back NaN.
Moved to `ζ = 3.0 + 4πi` (mid-strip), which has reliable coverage
at both tight and loose tolerances.

### M2 doesn't bite FF4.1.5 — the test footer predicted otherwise

The pre-write hypothesis for M2 (γ ↔ -δ swap, "wrong tronquée") was
that the pole-free sector at `Re ζ ≈ 3.5` would disappear → FF4.1.5
RED.  Empirically: M2 produces `u(3.5+0i) = -1.111` — the principal-
sheet asymptotic sector survives the parameter swap (the local
behavior at this specific sample is preserved by coincidence; the
sector globally would shift but our pin is a single point).

The FF4.1.3 / FF4.1.4 absolute pins bite M2 catastrophically (|Δ|
0.73 / 2.21).  Footer updated to document the actual empirical bite
pattern rather than the pre-write narrative.  Worklog 037's lesson:
"mutation bite is empirical; the footer should document WHICH
assertion each mutation actually bites, not which one we expected
it to."  Lesson re-confirmed on Fig 4.

## Hard-won lesson

**The off-IC oracle problem generalizes.**  Worklog 036 (Fig 6) ran
into loose-vs-tight working well on its three sheets because the
ζ-window was narrow (`Re ζ ∈ [-1, 2.5]`, IC at `Re ζ = 0` near the
center).  Worklog 037 (Fig 1) discovered the gap and switched to
FFW md:122 conjugate-symmetry for sheet 0 + qualitative pins for
sheets ±1.  Fig 4 sits between these: the IC at `Re ζ ≈ 3.40` is at
the *boundary* of the ζ-window, not the center, so even sheet 0
**far** from the IC is off-tree-topology.

The general rule emerging across the three FFW PV/PIII figures:

  - **Sheet-0 near-IC** sample (`|Δζ| < 1` from IC): loose-vs-tight
    works.  Use it as the controller-discipline detector.
  - **Sheet-0 far** or **any non-IC sheet** sample: loose-vs-tight
    captures tree-topology noise.  Use a captured tight-tol
    absolute baseline instead — bites physical-problem mutations.
  - **Pole-free sector** physical features: qualitative pins
    (finite + bounded + smooth-phase ring) catch sector-destroying
    mutations.
  - **Pole-density structure**: count gradients catch density-
    flattening mutations.

A future FFW PVI figure (Figs 2, 3, 7) will need this same multi-
oracle layering; the four-class assertion pattern (FF*.1.1 IC pin,
FF*.1.2-4 sheet pins, FF*.1.5 sector qualitative, FF*.1.6 density
gradient) is the right template.

## Empirical results

Achieved per-sheet quantitative pin summary:

  | sheet | FFW (md:236) | this work | threshold (1 order looser) | oracle             |
  |-------|--------------|-----------|----------------------------|--------------------|
  | 0     | 3e-10        | 0.0       | 3e-9                       | loose-vs-tight     |
  | 1     | 7e-7         | 0.0       | 7e-6                       | abs tight baseline |
  | 2     | 1e-6         | 0.0       | 1e-5                       | abs tight baseline |

The sheet-0 and abs-pin numbers being **exactly 0.0** is not a
mistake: the figure's `1e-10` tolerance produces a Stage-2 lookup
hitting the SAME visited node as the `1e-13` reference at the
near-IC sample (FF4.1.2 sheet 0), AND the tight-tol baseline is
captured FROM the same run that the test re-creates (FF4.1.3 /
FF4.1.4 absolute pins).  This is reproducibility, not a numerical
coincidence.

Pole-free-sector signature (FF4.1.5): `u(ζ=3.5+0i) = -1.046 +
1.2e-16 i`, max ring `|Δarg u|` = 2.26e-3, well inside the
`< π/4` no-pole criterion.  Quantitative confirmation of FFW
md:209's `u → -1, z → ∞ for -π < arg z < π`.

Pole-density gradient (FF4.1.6): 266 poles in `Re ζ ∈ [3, 4]` vs 32
in `[1, 2]` on sheets 1+2 (~8× ratio).  Confirms FFW md:72.

Wall time: Stage 1 ~25 s + Stage 2 ~1 s = **26 s** for the figure
script (single-threaded, order = 30, Float64).  Comparable to FFW's
own timings — though FFW's exact numbers aren't quoted for Fig 4.

## What is NOT shipped

  - **Asymptotic-series IC reconstruction**.  FFW md:209 says the
    IC `(u(30), u'(30)) = (-1.05294..., 2.47e-3)` was obtained by
    "substituting `u = Σ a_n z^{-n}` into P_V" and evaluating at
    `z = 30`.  We use FFW's published numerical values verbatim; we
    do NOT reconstruct the asymptotic-series solve.  Per the bead
    spec this is a deferred concern.  A future bead would add
    `painleveseries.jl`-style recursive coefficient generation for
    PV tronquée + an `aspot_eval(parameters, N, z)` evaluator.

  - **Double-multiplicity zero verification**.  FFW md:207: "all the
    zeros of the solution have double multiplicity" identified by
    the "color-wheel-twice" pattern.  Our phase portrait Column 3
    *shows* this pattern but we do not extract the zeros and verify
    their multiplicity.  This would require a contour-integration-
    based zero-counting primitive over the phase portrait,
    counting `arg u` winding modulo `4π` (vs `2π` for simple zeros).
    Deferred — the pole-density gradient (FF4.1.6) is the load-
    bearing structural check for now.

  - **FFW Re ζ ∈ [-1, 4] vs the published full window**.  FFW's
    actual Fig 4 figure may render a wider domain (FFW md:236 does
    not state dimensions explicitly).  Our reduced window covers
    the IC at `ζ₀ ≈ 3.40` plus enough on either side to show the
    tronquée + sheet structure.  Widening is an aesthetic
    improvement, not a correctness one.
